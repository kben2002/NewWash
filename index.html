New Washington Kalibo, Aklan

New Washington, officially the Municipality of New Washington (Aklanon: Banwa it New Washington; Hiligaynon: Banwa sang New Washington;
Tagalog: Bayan ng New Washington), is a 3rd class municipality in the province of Aklan, Philippines. According to the 2020 census, it has a population of 47,955 people. New Washington is particularly known for being the home town of Cardinal Jaime Sin, former Archbishop of Manila.

About New Washington

ETYMOLOGY

Established on January 15, 1904, the municipality was named after the first president of the United States, George Washington, as a tribute to the Thomasites, a group of American teachers who in the early 1900s established a new public education system in the Philippines, taught basic education, and trained Filipino teachers with English as medium of instruction. The municipality was formerly called Fonda Lagatic, which was derived from Lagatik River that stretches along some of the municipality's barangays at a length of 9.6 kilometers (6.0 mi).

Education In N.W

All the barangays have either an elementary or primary school. There are four secondary schools: New Washington National Comprehensive High School and Montfort Technical Institute (MTI) in Poblacion,
Candelaria National High School in Candelaria and Ochando National High School in Ochando. Montessori de Aklan (MDA),a primary school,is a branch of Montessori de Zamboanga Schools which is an affiliate of AMS or American Montessori Society in Polo,
Tertiary education is centered at Aklan State University - College of Fisheries and Marine Sciences, which is located in Poblacion. Vocational courses are also offered at MTI. Some families send their children to schools in Kalibo, Capiz, Iloilo and Manila, among others.

Montfort Technical Institute

The Montfort Technical Institute (MTI) is a non-profit Catholic educational institution administered by the Montfort Brothers of St. Gabriel. With schools in over 30 countries, MTI is committed to facilitating the integral formation of youth through vocational training centers for underprivileged boys and girls. Located in Poblacion, New Washington, Aklan, MTI offers grade school to senior high school and technical/vocational courses such as Automotive Servicing and Beauty Care Services.
It is recognized by DepEd, accredited with FAPE and TESDA, and aims to provide quality education while fostering appreciation for science, culture, and arts. The school has 50 regular staff, 3 project-based staff, 4 regular volunteers, and 5 members of the Board of Trustees.

Aklan State University

The Aklan State University - New Washington is one of the campuses under the Aklan State University (ASU) system. It offers undergraduate programs in Fisheries, Customs Administration, Criminology, Marine Biology, Secondary Education and Hotel and Restaurant Management.
Also available is a master's program in Education that focuses on Physical Education. Like its fellow campuses, the ASU - New Washington campus is duly recognized by the Commission on Higher Education (CHED) and is active in developing its students into competent professionals instrumental for nation-building.

New Washington National Comprehensive High School

The New Washington Comprehensive National High School (NWACHS) is a public secondary comprehensive school of the City of Kalibo, Aklan. It has two campuses, the main and the annex.
The annex campus is located behind the Norkis Park and access to the other side can be had by way of a covered pedestrian overpass which spanned DM Cortes St. The main campus is situated between the Engineer's Office Building and the Department of Education Aklan City Division Building

BARANGAY'S

New Washington is politically subdivided into 16 barangays. To some locals, the barangays are grouped into two, namely "Eanas" and "Baybay".
The barangays that belong to Eanas are those involved in rice farming such as Mabilo, Puis, Jalas, Guinbaliwan, Mataphao, Candelaria, Lawa-an and Jugas.
Baybay, on the other hand, covers barangays along or near the coastline, consisting of Tambac, Poblacion, Pinamuk-an, Polo, Cawayan, Ochando, Fatima and Dumaguit. Common among municipalities in the Philippines, the seat of local government and the center of business are situated in Poblacion. It is also home to the Municipal Auditorium and Sports Complex, where major events of the town are held.
